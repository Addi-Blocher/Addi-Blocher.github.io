Film and Digital Photography Examples

I like to find beauty in the every day, whether I'm writing, designing, or photographing. This point of view has gotten me through some of my personal difficulty, and I think that it's a helpful mindset for those of us who struggle a little more in our own minds. Enjoying the small things and being able to find beauty and intrigue wherever I'm at is what I aim to do, always.

We were driving up into the rocky mountains, and it was a mystically cloudy day. I wanted to highlight the overwhelming greyness of the day, so I pulled color in my color grade.
I took a few of the same picture with different camera settings. The ultimate goal was to balance the darker background against the bright lights without overexposing or underexposing anything. This one came out the best of them all!
I just love the skyline. It's hard not to, right?
My wonderful friend sat for me through the fire. She was very patient!
The hues inside of red wine fascinate me, and the light of the fire really helped to bring them out. I wanted to show the rich red that exists in wine.
The positions that you find yourself in playing pool (not very well if you're anything like me) are fairly unique to that activity. The slant of the shoulders makes a line that cuts through the image and allows for a simplicity and beautiful profile.
The depth of field down a pool table creates for an interesting telescope effect in this image. His posture and the line of the pool cue pull focus to his face in a very unique way.
The way that the model's hair is falling over his face gives him this look of determination, like he has blinders on. He feels isolated from the camera and incredibly comeptent and pool.
I walked into this record store and immediately needed to capture the clutered complexity of the interior. I think that the posters on the ceiling are a great way to highlight that, like they had so much they wanted to put up that it couldn't fit on the walls and the shelves alone. The only place left to go was up.
This was more about capturing the beauty that I was enjoying than creating art. The skyline was too colorful to resist.
