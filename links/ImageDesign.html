Glitch

This design was meant to feel disorienting.

Lungs

The lungs are meant to look like the old version of 3D designs, with the red and blue, but instead purple and green.

Repeat

Here, I wanted to make this feel like sinking calmly. Like you have no idea which way is up, but it's okay, because why does it matter anyways?

Scream

I wanted this to feel like the photo design version of a glass cracking when someone screams at just the right pitch.

Space

When I was young, the words 'space cadet' were thrown at me like an insult. I was diagnosed with ADHD-I when I was nine, and that explained some of it. I wanted this to reflect those words that had been used for me when I was young.

Whales

I wanted to make the human mind seem large. Comparing it whales, at the same size or even larger, allowed me to make the human intellect seem like the biggest thing on earth.

Stones

This was meant to look like the highlights and lowlights of the stone stacks that I've often seen on the side of hiking trails. They've got a specific kind of peace to them.

Bubbles

I just really like bubbles. Don't you? They have so many colors, and the way that they refelct the same thing regardless of size is just beautiful.

Details

I think this reflects some of the midcentury design that has been coming back into vogue recently. I like adding highlights to natural features. I think that's always what portraiture should be about, not necessarily changing a person but adding emphasis to the best parts of them.
