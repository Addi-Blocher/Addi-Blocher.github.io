This was pattern was by Lisas Attik on Etsy. I liked the clear division between pattern changes, and this blue yarn was calling my name.
This is the brighter later shawl by WoolnHook on Etsy. I wanted a nice triangle shawl for fall, and this lovely brown and cream gradient yarn was perfect for the task.
This sweater pattern was by YarnCakesLLC on Etsy. I adapted it to be the correct size for me, and to loosen the turtleneck. I liked the patterning of the body and the sleeves.
I adapted this pattern from a vintage granny square coat that fell at the waist. I liked the idea of the three ties at the top, because when I walk it flares open in a really nice way.
The pattern for this dress was from Mermaid Cat Designs on Etsy. I liked the open back and the way that it moves.
Here's that lovely open back I was talking about. I chose green for the dress, and the pearl white buttons to accent the lovely forest color.
Here's the detailing in the dress skirt. There's one of these details on the left side. There were over 300 stitches per round in the skirt by the time you reach the bottom of the pattern.
This pattern was by RomanDyreDesigns on Etsy. I had to adapt the pattern to work the way that I wanted to. The original stitch used didn't move the way that I like, so I changed to a regular double crochet which is more flexible.
